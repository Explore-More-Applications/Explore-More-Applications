Pharmaceuticals

Why Pharmaceuticals Matter

  • Sri Lanka imports over 85% of its pharmaceutical needs, costing hundreds of millions in foreign currency.
  • The government and private sector aim to grow domestic manufacturing to reduce dependency and enhance national health security.
  • The industry contributes to job creation, FDI attraction, and export expansion through herbal-based products and essential generics.

 

Chemistry in Pharmaceuticals - Core to Every Pill

Chemistry is the foundation of every medicine - from natural plant extraction to precision drug design.

 

Key Areas Where Chemistry Drives Pharma:

Stage

Chemistry Involvement

Formulation

Mixing active ingredients (APIs) with excipients to ensure proper dosage, stability, and absorption

Synthesis & Extraction

Isolation of active molecules from plants or synthesis from chemical reactions

Quality Control

Ensures safety, purity, and bioavailability using analytical techniques like HPLC, FT-IR, UV-Vis

Drug Delivery

Chemists develop nanoparticles, liposomes, gels for targeted, slow-release, or non-invasive treatments

 

v  Liposomes are tiny, spherical vesicles with a lipid bilayer structure, similar to cell membranes, that can encapsulate both hydrophilic and hydrophobic substances. They are used in a variety of applications, including drug delivery, gene therapy, and cosmetics. 

 

v  Gels, particularly hydrogels and in-situ gels, are valuable tools for targeted drug delivery due to their ability to encapsulate and release drugs in a controlled manner

It's used as a folk medicine for treating diabetes, and several clinical trials have proven that it's effective as a treatment for type II diabetes

Future Green Approach in Pharma

To align health and sustainability, chemists are enabling greener, safer pharmaceutical innovations:

Green Chemistry for Herbal Pharma

  • Supercritical CO₂ Extraction: Replace solvents with CO₂ for clean, residue-free herbal extracts.
  • Biodegradable Packaging: Replace PVC/PET with starch-based or cellulose-based blister packs and bottles.

Waste-to-Value Conversion

  • Reuse pharmaceutical production waste (e.g., plant biomass) as biofertilizer or fuel.
  • Recover valuable byproducts like flavonoids and alkaloids from plant waste.

AI-Enhanced Drug Discovery

  • Chemists and data scientists use AI to model molecule-target interactions, reducing lab waste, cost, and time.
  • Improves clinical trial success rates by predicting side effects early.

 

 

 

Cosmeceuticals

 

Cosmeceuticals & Chemistry: Enhancing Sri Lankas Beauty Economy

 

Why Cosmeceuticals Matter

  • Cosmeceuticals combine cosmetics with active pharmaceutical-grade ingredients providing therapeutic benefits beyond basic skincare.
  • Global demand is rapidly growing, projected to reach billions globally, presenting significant export opportunities for Sri Lanka.
  • Local brands like Spa Ceylon leverage Sri Lankas natural biodiversity, cultural heritage, and traditional Ayurvedic knowledge, creating a unique global appeal.

 

Chemistry Behind Cosmeceuticals

Cosmeceuticals rely on the extraction, isolation, and formulation of active natural compounds. Chemists meticulously ensure bioactivity, stability, and safety.

Real-world Examples from Sri Lanka

1. Spa Ceylon - Ayurveda Luxury

  • Uses scientifically validated natural extracts (sandalwood, aloe, turmeric) in skincare and wellness products.
  • Chemists at Spa Ceylon standardize extracts for consistency, effectiveness, and global regulatory compliance.
  • Exported to over 20 countries, Spa Ceylon showcases Sri Lankas traditional herbal wisdom combined with modern chemistry.

 

2. Agarwood Sri Lankas Luxury Ingredient

  • Chemists extract rare oils from agarwood (Aquilaria trees) containing sesquiterpenes, valuable for luxury perfumes and anti-aging creams.
  • Due to high global demand, Sri Lankan agarwood fetches premium export prices, significantly contributing to foreign income.

3. Sandalwood Heritage Ingredient

  • Sri Lankan chemists isolate sandalwoods key actives (α and β-santalol) for cosmetics used in anti-aging, calming products, and high-end fragrances.
  • A significant economic contributor, sandalwood-derived products boost tourism and exports, enhancing Sri Lankas global cosmetic profile.

 

Chemists' Economic Contributions in Sri Lankan Cosmeceuticals

Product Development & Innovation

  • Create innovative formulations integrating traditional herbs and cutting-edge chemistry, ensuring global appeal and marketability.

Quality Assurance & Regulatory Compliance

  • Guarantee safety, stability, purity, and efficacy, crucial for export to strict international markets (e.g., EU, USA, Japan).

Brand & Export Market Expansion

  • Chemists scientifically validate claims, boosting consumer trust and market competitiveness (e.g., Spa Ceylon's global market expansion).

Economic & Employment Growth

  • Stimulate local economies via cultivation of raw materials (herbs, sandalwood, agarwood), processing industries, and export-oriented SMEs.

 

Future Green Approach for Cosmeceuticals

Sustainable Ingredient Sourcing

  • Develop ethical agroforestry practices for agarwood and sandalwood, preventing deforestation and ensuring long-term supply.

Green Extraction Technologies

  • Employ supercritical CO₂ extraction, ultrasound-assisted extraction (UAE), and microwave-assisted extraction (MAE), reducing chemical use and environmental impact.

Eco-Friendly Packaging

  • Shift from plastics to biodegradable, compostable packaging (starch-based, cellulose-based), enhancing sustainability credentials and marketability.

Zero-Waste Production

  • Chemists can convert extraction residues into valuable secondary products like biofertilizers, biofuels, or natural colorants.
